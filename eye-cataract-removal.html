Frequently Asked Questions

Can you see anything during eye surgery?

During eye surgery, such as LASIK, patients may experience light and shadow but typically do not see detailed images due to the use of numbing drops and other medications that help keep the eye comfortable and vision obscured.

Are you awake for eye surgery?

In most cases, patients are awake during eye surgery, including LASIK. However, they are typically given medications to help them relax, and numbing drops are applied to the eyes to minimize discomfort.

What is the regret rate for LASIK eye surgery?

The regret rate for LASIK is relatively low, with the majority of patients experiencing satisfaction with their results. Factors contributing to regret may include unrealistic expectations, complications, or dissatisfaction with the outcome.
